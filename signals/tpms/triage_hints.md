# Triage Hints: TPMS (Tire Pressure Monitoring System)

These hints help the LLM identify and verify TPMS signals from statistical reports generated by `triage_iq.py`.

---

## 🔍 Key Spectral & Statistical Indicators

### 1. Center Frequency
* TPMS transmits at precisely **433.92 MHz** (Europe/Asia) or **315 MHz** (North America).
* If the capture center frequency is within ±500 kHz of these bands and the signal characteristics match below, TPMS is a strong candidate.

### 2. Occupied Bandwidth (OBW)
* The 99% OBW is typically **60 kHz to 100 kHz**.
* This is set by twice the frequency deviation plus the symbol rate: $OBW \approx 2 \Delta f + R_{sym} \approx 2 \times 38 + 10 = 86\ \text{kHz}$ (Carson's rule).
* If the OBW is significantly wider (>200 kHz), it is likely not TPMS.

### 3. Constant Envelope (Low PAPR) — FSK Sensors
* Modern TPMS sensors use FSK, which is a constant-envelope modulation.
* **PAPR** during active bursts: **< 1.5 dB** (typically < 0.8 dB).
* **Normalized Amplitude Std/Mean**: Extremely low (**< 0.05**).
* *Exception*: Older OOK/ASK-based sensors will have higher PAPR (3-6 dB) and significant amplitude variation (Std/Mean > 0.4).

### 4. SNR
* Typical received SNR at close range (< 10 m): **15 dB to 30 dB**.
* At longer ranges or through vehicle body panels: **8 dB to 15 dB**.
* If the SNR is below 8 dB, reliable decoding may be difficult without matched filtering.

---

## ⏱ Key Temporal Indicators

### 1. Burst Duration
* TPMS packets are very short: **2 ms to 8 ms** active burst duration.
* This is significantly shorter than most other 433 MHz ISM protocols (weather stations: 30-100 ms, key fobs: 10-50 ms).

### 2. Duty Cycle
* **Extremely low duty cycle**: < 0.1%.
* Sensors transmit approximately once every **30 to 60 seconds** while driving.
* In a 60-second capture, you will typically see only **1 to 3 bursts** (some sensors repeat the packet 2-4 times within a ~100 ms window).

### 3. Burst Repetition Pattern
* Some TPMS sensors transmit a cluster of 2-4 identical packets spaced ~20-30 ms apart, followed by a long silence period. Look for this distinctive **micro-burst clustering** pattern in the spectrogram or temporal envelope.

---

## 🧬 Autocorrelation Periodicities

### Symbol Rate Detection
* Autocorrelation of the **FM-demodulated signal** (not the raw IQ envelope) will reveal peaks at the symbol clock period:
  $$T_{sym} = \frac{1}{R_{sym}} = \frac{1}{10000} = 100\ \mu\text{s}$$
* At a sample rate of **2.4 MSPS**, this corresponds to a lag of **240 samples**.
* Look for a sharp, repeating autocorrelation peak at lag **240 samples** (or integer multiples: 480, 720, ...).

### Preamble Detection
* The alternating `010101...` preamble creates a very strong periodic pattern at half the symbol period ($T_{sym}/2 = 50\ \mu\text{s}$, or **120 samples** at 2.4 MSPS).

---

## ⚠️ Common False Positives

### 1. Weather Stations (433 MHz)
* Weather stations (e.g., Oregon Scientific, Acurite, LaCrosse) also operate at 433.92 MHz with FSK or OOK modulation.
* **Differentiators**: Weather station packets are **much longer** (30-100 ms vs. 2-8 ms for TPMS), use different sync words, and contain larger payloads (humidity, wind speed, rainfall data). Their repetition rate is typically every 30-60 seconds but with longer active transmission windows.

### 2. Garage Door Remotes / Key Fobs (433 MHz)
* Remote controls commonly use **OOK** (not FSK), so check the PAPR and amplitude variation.
* Key fob bursts are typically longer (10-50 ms) and use rolling code schemes with different frame lengths.

### 3. ISM Sensor Networks
* General 433 MHz ISM sensors (soil moisture, pool thermometers, etc.) share the band.
* **Differentiator**: TPMS packets are among the shortest in the band. Look for the combination of very short burst + very low duty cycle + specific sync word patterns.
