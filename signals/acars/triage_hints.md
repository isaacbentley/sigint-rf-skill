# Triage Hints: ACARS — Aircraft Communications Addressing and Reporting System

These hints help the LLM identify and verify ACARS signals from statistical reports generated by `triage_iq.py`.

---

## 🔍 Key Spectral Indicators

### 1. Frequency Band
* ACARS operates in the **VHF aviation band (118–137 MHz)** — the same band used for ATC voice communications.
* Primary frequencies to check:
  - **129.125 MHz** — worldwide primary
  - **130.025 MHz** — USA
  - **131.550 MHz** — European / global primary
  - **136.700, 136.800, 136.900 MHz** — secondary/overflow
* If your capture covers any of these frequencies, ACARS is a candidate.

### 2. Occupied Bandwidth (OBW)
* ACARS appears as a **narrowband AM signal** occupying approximately **6–8 kHz**.
* The AM carrier is clearly visible as a strong spectral spike at the channel center.
* MSK data appears as two symmetric humps around the carrier at ±1200 Hz and ±2400 Hz offsets.
* This is significantly **narrower** than VDL Mode 2 (~25 kHz) or ATC voice (~8.33 kHz channel).

### 3. AM Carrier Presence
* Unlike GMSK or PSK signals, ACARS has a **strong residual carrier** (AM full-carrier mode).
* The PSD will show a **prominent spectral line at the center frequency** with data sidebands.
* This is a key distinguisher from digital-only signals.

---

## ⏱ Key Temporal Indicators

### 1. Burst Characteristics
* ACARS transmissions are **event-driven and sporadic** — not periodic like AIS TDMA.
* Typical burst durations:
  - **Short messages** (OOOI events, acknowledgments): **50–100 ms**
  - **Medium messages** (weather, clearances): **100–200 ms**
  - **Long messages** (free text, ACARS over HF relay): **200–800 ms**
* Bursts appear as isolated data "chirps" on AM voice frequencies.

### 2. Preamble Signature
* Every ACARS burst begins with a **6.67 ms alternating-polarity preamble** (16 bits at 2400 baud).
* This creates a brief ~1200 Hz tone at the start of each burst — a distinctive audio "warble" before the data content begins.

### 3. Duty Cycle
* Duty cycle is **very low (<1%)** in most captures — ACARS transmits only on specific events (gate departure, liftoff, etc.).
* Near busy airports, multiple aircraft may produce intermittent bursts every few seconds.
* PAPR of the full capture will be **high** due to sparse AM bursts in silence.

---

## 🧬 Autocorrelation Periodicities

### Symbol-Level
* MSK at 2400 baud produces a symbol period of:
$$T_{sym} = \frac{1}{2400} \approx 416.67\ \mu\text{s}$$
* At **48 kSPS** (after AM demod / audio resampling): lag of **20 samples**.
* At **250 kSPS** (RF sample rate): lag of **104 samples**.
* A strong autocorrelation peak at the symbol period confirms 2400 baud.

### Tone-Level
* The two MSK tones (1200 Hz and 2400 Hz) will produce spectral peaks in the demodulated audio:
  - 1200 Hz → period of 833 µs
  - 2400 Hz → period of 417 µs
* Look for these in the audio-domain PSD after AM demodulation.

## ❌ Common False Positives

| Confused With | How to Distinguish |
|---|---|
| **ATC Voice (AM)** | ATC voice is continuous AM speech with formant structure. ACARS is digital data with fixed MSK tones — no voice-like spectral content. Look for the 1200/2400 Hz tone pair. |
| **ATIS broadcast** | ATIS is synthesized voice on AM. ACARS is data-only with no speech content. |
| **VDL Mode 2 (136.725/136.975 MHz)** | VDL2 uses D8PSK at 10500 bps — much wider bandwidth (~25 kHz), no AM carrier, and different frequencies. ACARS is AM+MSK at only 2400 baud with a strong AM carrier. |
| **ELT (121.5 MHz)** | ELT is a continuous sweeping tone (distress beacon). Completely different from ACARS bursty data. |
| **HFDL (HF ACARS)** | HFDL operates in the HF band (2–22 MHz), not VHF. Uses OFDM-like modulation. Different frequency range entirely. |
| **CPDLC / FANS** | CPDLC messages may be carried over ACARS or VDL2. If the underlying physical layer is ACARS AM+MSK, these hints still apply. |

## ✅ High-Confidence ACARS Identification Checklist

1. ☐ Center frequency matches a known ACARS channel (129.125, 130.025, 130.450, 131.125, 131.550, 136.700, 136.800, 136.900 MHz)
2. ☐ AM carrier visible as a strong spectral line at channel center
3. ☐ Narrowband signal (~6–8 kHz occupied bandwidth)
4. ☐ Bursty transmission pattern — isolated events, not continuous
5. ☐ Burst durations in the 50–800 ms range
6. ☐ Audio-domain analysis reveals 1200 Hz and 2400 Hz MSK tones
7. ☐ Symbol rate measurement near 2400 baud (autocorrelation at ~417 µs)
8. ☐ Each burst begins with alternating-polarity preamble (~6.67 ms)
