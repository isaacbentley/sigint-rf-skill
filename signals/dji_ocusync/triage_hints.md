# Triage Hints: DJI OcuSync (O1–O4)

These hints help the LLM identify and verify DJI OcuSync signals from statistical reports generated by `triage_iq.py`.

---

## 🔍 Key Spectral Indicators

### 1. Occupied Bandwidth (OBW)
OcuSync operates in distinct bandwidth modes. Look for these canonical 99% OBW values:
* **10 MHz Mode**: Measured OBW between **9.0 MHz and 9.8 MHz** (Most common mode for DroneID telemetry captures).
* **20 MHz Mode**: Measured OBW between **18.0 MHz and 19.5 MHz**.
* **40 MHz Mode**: Measured OBW between **36.0 MHz and 39.0 MHz**.

### 2. High PAPR (Peak-to-Average Power Ratio)
* Because OFDM signals are the sum of hundreds of independent subcarriers, they exhibit a high **PAPR of 8.0 dB to 12.0 dB**.
* If the PAPR is low (< 5.0 dB), it is likely *not* OFDM and therefore not OcuSync.

### 3. OFDM Flatness Score
* OcuSync PSD has steep roll-offs and a flat top. The flatness score computed by `triage_iq.py` will typically be **> 0.75**.

---

## ⏱ Key Temporal Indicators

### 1. Burst Duration & Duty Cycle
* OcuSync is packetized/bursty in nature. The duty cycle is usually low (**5% to 30%**).
* Typical active burst durations are **1.4 ms to 5.0 ms** (e.g., $1.43\ \text{ms}$ or $2.86\ \text{ms}$, representing LTE slot intervals of 2 or 4 subframes).

### 2. Guard Band / Burst Spacing
* Bursts occur periodically. You will often see periodic peaks in the envelope at intervals of **10 ms, 20 ms, or 40 ms**.

---

## 🧬 Autocorrelation Periodicities

If the sample rate is configured to **15.36 MSPS**:
* Look for a distinct autocorrelation peak at lag **1024 samples** (useful symbol period $T_u \approx 66.67\ \mu\text{s}$) or **1096-1100 samples** (total symbol period with cyclic prefix $T_s \approx 71.35\ \mu\text{s}$).
* At **30.72 MSPS**, the peak will double to **2048 / 2192-2200 samples**.

---

## 🛡 Common False Positives / Exclusions

1. **802.11 Wi-Fi**:
   * Wi-Fi also uses OFDM (20 MHz and 40 MHz) and has similar PAPR/Flatness.
   * *Differentiation*: Wi-Fi packets have highly variable lengths (often very short, < 1 ms) and variable spacing. OcuSync bursts have deterministic, highly periodic spacing (10 ms, 20 ms bounds) and carry the Zadoff-Chu synchronization peaks at root 600 and 147 which Wi-Fi lacks.
2. **Bluetooth (FHSS)**:
   * Bluetooth hoppers are narrow (1 MHz OBW) and hop rapidly.
   * *Differentiation*: Bluetooth occupied bandwidth is strictly 1 MHz, whereas OcuSync is always $\ge 1.4\ \text{MHz}$ (usually 10 or 20 MHz).
