# Triage Hints: FPV Analog Video (PAL / NTSC)

These hints help the LLM identify and verify FPV Analog video signals from statistical reports generated by `triage_iq.py`.

---

## 🔍 Key Spectral Indicators

### 1. Occupied Bandwidth (OBW)
* Standard analog FPV signals exhibit an OBW (99%) between **5.5 MHz and 8.0 MHz**.
* The bandwidth varies slightly depending on video detail (high frequency detail increases FM sidebands).

### 2. Extremely Low PAPR (Peak-to-Average Power Ratio)
* Wideband FM signals have a constant envelope in the time domain. 
* PAPR is **strictly between 0.0 dB and 1.5 dB** (typically <1.0 dB). This is the single strongest indicator distinguishing analog FPV from digital signals like Wi-Fi or OcuSync, which have PAPR values > 8.0 dB.

### 3. OFDM Flatness Score
* Because it is a single carrier FM signal, the spectrum does not have a flat top. The flatness score will be **low (< 0.4)**.

---

## ⏱ Key Temporal Indicators

### 1. Duty Cycle
* FPV analog video is continuous. The duty cycle is **100%** (always active, no pauses, no packetization).

---

## 🧬 Autocorrelation Periodicities (Line Synchronization)

Because the CVBS signal repeats synchronization pulses once per horizontal line, the autocorrelation of the demodulated FM signal exhibits strong peaks.
Look for peaks in the autocorrelation matching these specific line durations:

* **PAL standard**: Peak at lag equivalent to **$64.0\ \mu\text{s}$**.
* **NTSC standard**: Peak at lag equivalent to **$63.56\ \mu\text{s}$**.

### Sample Lag Reference Table:

| Sample Rate (MSPS) | Expected PAL Lag (samples) | Expected NTSC Lag (samples) |
|---|---|---|
| **15.36 MSPS** | ~983 samples | ~976 samples |
| **20.00 MSPS** | 1280 samples | 1271 samples |
| **25.00 MSPS** | 1600 samples | 1589 samples |
| **30.72 MSPS** | ~1966 samples | ~1952 samples |

If the triage report displays a strong correlation peak at these lag bounds, it indicates analog video, and the exact lag determines PAL vs. NTSC.

---

## 🛠 Demodulation Tooling Notes

* **`explainable_demod.py` Limitations**: Be aware that the local `explainable_demod.py` script uses "blind" rasterization for analog video modes. Because it does not mathematically lock onto H-Sync pulses or decode the color burst subcarrier, the resulting frames will be grayscale, slanted, and severely stretched. Use this as an educational point for the operator: while it confirms the presence of video (luma), a dedicated tool like `tvsharp` or a GNU Radio CVBS block is required for proper color and sync recovery.
