# Triage Hints: Pager Systems (POCSAG & FLEX)

These hints help the LLM identify and verify POCSAG and FLEX paging signals from statistical reports generated by `triage_iq.py`.

---

## 🔍 Key Spectral Indicators

### 1. Occupied Bandwidth (OBW)
* POCSAG signals occupy a narrow channel:
  - **25 kHz** (legacy) or **12.5 kHz** (narrowband) channel spacing.
  - 99% OBW is approximately **8–15 kHz** depending on baud rate and deviation.
* FLEX signals similarly occupy **~15–20 kHz** within a 25 kHz channel.
* If the OBW exceeds 25 kHz, it is not a standard paging signal.

### 2. Low PAPR (Peak-to-Average Power Ratio)
* FSK is a constant-envelope modulation scheme.
* PAPR should be **very low (~1.0–2.5 dB)** — similar to FM signals.
* High PAPR (>5 dB) rules out POCSAG/FLEX.

### 3. Spectral Shape
* 2-FSK produces a **twin-peaked** power spectral density centered on the carrier, with peaks at ±4.5 kHz (POCSAG) or ±4.8 kHz (FLEX).
* 4-FSK (FLEX 6400) shows **four spectral lobes** at ±1.6 kHz and ±4.8 kHz.
* OFDM flatness score will be **very low (<0.3)** — this is a single-carrier signal.

## ⏱ Key Temporal Indicators

### 1. Preamble Duration (POCSAG — Smoking Gun)
The POCSAG preamble is **extremely long** and distinctive:

| Baud Rate | Preamble Duration |
|---|---|
| **512 baud** | **1.125 s** |
| **1200 baud** | **480 ms** |
| **2400 baud** | **240 ms** |

* A burst starting with a sustained, perfectly periodic tone (alternating bits at the baud rate) lasting hundreds of milliseconds is a near-certain POCSAG indicator.
* The preamble appears as a pure tone at half the baud rate (e.g., 256 Hz tone for 512 baud, 600 Hz for 1200 baud).

### 2. Burst Structure
* POCSAG transmissions are **bursty** — short messages with long idle periods.
* Typical burst duration: **200 ms to 3 seconds** (preamble + 1–10 batches).
* Duty cycle is **very low (<1%)** for individual pagers.
* Base stations may transmit near-continuously during busy periods.

### 3. FLEX Timing
* FLEX frames are rigidly **1.875 seconds** each.
* If bursts repeat at exactly 1.875 s intervals, suspect FLEX.
* FLEX cycle = 128 frames = exactly **4 minutes**.

## 🧬 Autocorrelation Periodicities

### POCSAG Preamble Periodicity
During the preamble, the alternating `10101...` pattern creates a strong autocorrelation peak at exactly **2 bit periods**:

| Baud Rate | Bit Period | Autocorrelation Peak (at 2 MSPS) |
|---|---|---|
| **512 baud** | 1.953 ms | **3906 samples** |
| **1200 baud** | 0.833 ms | **1667 samples** |
| **2400 baud** | 0.417 ms | **833 samples** |

### Batch Periodicity
After the preamble, each batch consists of sync (32 bits) + 16 codewords (512 bits) = **544 bits** total:

| Baud Rate | Batch Duration |
|---|---|
| **512 baud** | 1.0625 s |
| **1200 baud** | 453 ms |
| **2400 baud** | 227 ms |

## ⚠️ Common False Positives

| Confounder | How to Distinguish |
|---|---|
| **AFSK data (AX.25, APRS)** | AFSK uses audio tones (1200/2200 Hz) on FM; POCSAG is direct FSK on the carrier. Check center frequency — APRS is on 144.390 MHz (US). |
| **Narrowband FM voice** | FM voice has variable amplitude envelope and wider spectral spread; POCSAG has rigid periodic structure during preamble. |
| **TETRA / DMR** | Digital trunked radio uses 4FSK but with different deviation (±1944 Hz for DMR) and no long alternating preamble. |
| **Morse / CW** | Morse is on-off keyed; POCSAG is continuous FSK during transmission. |

## 🎯 Quick Decision Tree

```
1. Center frequency in 148–174 MHz, 450–470 MHz, or 929–932 MHz?
   └─ YES → Candidate paging band.
2. OBW ≤ 25 kHz and PAPR < 3 dB?
   └─ YES → Consistent with FSK paging.
3. Long sustained tone (>200 ms) of alternating bits at start of burst?
   └─ YES → Strong POCSAG indicator. Tone frequency reveals baud rate:
            • ~256 Hz → 512 baud
            • ~600 Hz → 1200 baud
            • ~1200 Hz → 2400 baud
4. Rigid 1.875 s frame timing at 929–932 MHz?
   └─ YES → FLEX protocol.
```
