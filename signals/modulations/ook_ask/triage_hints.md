# Triage Hints: OOK / ASK

These hints help the LLM identify and verify OOK and ASK signals from statistical reports generated by `triage_iq.py`.

---

## 🔍 Key Spectral & Statistical Indicators

### 1. High Amplitude Standard Deviation
* Unlike FSK or PSK which are constant-envelope (zero or minimal amplitude variance), ASK/OOK signals vary their amplitude to encode data.
* **Normalized Amplitude Standard Deviation (Std/Mean)**: Typically **$> 0.4$**.
* If amplitude variance is near zero, it is not ASK/OOK.

### 2. Peak-to-Average Power Ratio (PAPR)
* Because the carrier is entirely suppressed for '0' bits in OOK, the average power is half (or less) of the peak power.
* The measured PAPR during an active burst is typically **$3.0\text{ dB to } 6.0\text{ dB}$**.

### 3. Occupied Bandwidth
* Due to sharp transitions (square-like pulses in the time domain), ASK/OOK signals exhibit wide sidebands. The 99% OBW will be relatively large compared to the 90% OBW.

---

## ⏱ Key Temporal Indicators

### 1. Bursty Nature
* ASK/OOK signals are almost always bursty (e.g. key fobs transmitting only when a button is pressed).
* The duty cycle will be extremely low (**$< 1\%$**).
* Typical active burst durations range from **$5\text{ ms to } 100\text{ ms}$**.

### 2. Pulse Structure
* Zooming in on the temporal envelope reveals distinct high/low square levels. If using PWM, there will be two discrete pulse durations representing 1 and 0.

---

## 🧬 Autocorrelation Periodicities

Autocorrelation of the amplitude envelope will reveal the symbol rate (or pulse clock rate):
* Look for a sharp, repeating peak at lag $T_{sym}$.
* E.g. a key fob operating at 1 kbps (symbol duration $= 1\text{ ms}$) will show repeating autocorrelation peaks at lags equivalent to **$1.0\text{ ms}, 2.0\text{ ms}, 3.0\text{ ms}$**, etc.
* The presence of harmonic peaks (integer multiples) strongly confirms a periodic rectangular clock structure characteristic of ASK/OOK pulse sequences.
