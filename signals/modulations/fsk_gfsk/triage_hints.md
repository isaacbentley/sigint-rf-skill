# Triage Hints: FSK / GFSK

These hints help the LLM identify and verify FSK and GFSK signals from statistical reports generated by `triage_iq.py`.

---

## 🔍 Key Spectral & Statistical Indicators

### 1. Constant Envelope (Low PAPR)
* Frequency modulation does not alter signal amplitude.
* **Peak-to-Average Power Ratio (PAPR)** during active transmission is **strictly $< 1.5\text{ dB}$** (typically $< 0.8\text{ dB}$).
* **Normalized Amplitude Standard Deviation (Std/Mean)**: Extremely low (**$< 0.05$**).
* *Note*: If amplitude standard deviation is high, the signal is not raw FSK (unless corrupted by severe multipath fading).

### 2. Dual-Peak (Symmetric Horn) PSD Shape
* For 2-FSK with a high modulation index ($h \ge 1$), the PSD will display **two symmetric peaks** representing the binary frequency offsets:
  $$f_0 = f_c - \Delta f \quad \text{and} \quad f_1 = f_c + \Delta f$$
* The distance between the peaks is equal to $2\Delta f$ (twice the frequency deviation).
* *Note for GFSK / Low Modulation Index*: If Gaussian pulse shaping is applied or the modulation index is small ($h \approx 0.5$), the two peaks merge into a single, smooth rounded curve. However, the PAPR remains constant envelope.

---

## ⏱ Key Temporal Indicators

* FSK can be either continuous (e.g. broadcasting telemetry) or packetized (e.g. Bluetooth).
* In the time-domain envelope, the signal appears as a flat, continuous block of power during transmission, with no amplitude dips.

---

## 🧬 Autocorrelation Periodicities

* Auto-correlation of the amplitude envelope will not reveal anything useful because the amplitude is constant.
* However, auto-correlation of the **frequency demodulated signal** (the instantaneous phase derivative) will display a strong peak at the symbol clock lag:
  $$T_{sym} = \frac{1}{R_{sym}}$$
* If this lag is known or can be estimated, it directly yields the bit rate of the transmission.
