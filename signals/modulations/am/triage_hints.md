# Triage Hints: AM (Amplitude Modulation)

These hints help the LLM identify and verify Amplitude Modulation (AM) signals from statistical reports generated by `triage_iq.py`.

---

## 🔍 Key Spectral & Statistical Indicators

### 1. Occupied Bandwidth (OBW)
* AM broadcast signals occupy a strictly limited bandwidth:
  - **AM Broadcast**: `6 kHz to 10 kHz` centered inside a `9 kHz` (Europe) or `10 kHz` (US) channel spacing window.
  - **VHF Air Band**: `6 kHz to 10 kHz` of occupied audio bandwidth centered inside a `25 kHz` or `8.33 kHz` channel spacing.
* The PSD will show a strong carrier spike at the center with symmetric audio sidebands on either side.

### 2. High PAPR (Peak-to-Average Power Ratio)
* Unlike constant-envelope FSK/FM, AM signals have significant amplitude variation driven by the modulating audio content.
* **PAPR**: Typically **4 dB to 8 dB** depending on modulation depth and voice/audio dynamics.
* This is a crucial differentiator from FSK/FM signals which have PAPR < 1.5 dB.

### 3. Amplitude Coefficient of Variation (Std/Mean)
* Significant amplitude fluctuations reflecting the modulating voice waveform.
* **Normalized Amplitude Std/Mean**: Typically **0.1 to 0.4**.
* This distinguishes AM from OOK/ASK (which has Std/Mean > 0.4 with discrete on/off levels) and from FSK (which has Std/Mean < 0.05).

---

## ⏱ Key Temporal Indicators

### 1. Continuous Streaming Pattern
* AM voice signals are **continuous** while the transmitter is active (e.g., push-to-talk on aviation, or continuous broadcast on AM radio).
* There are no discrete data packets or bursty behavior — the temporal envelope smoothly varies with speech or music content.

### 2. Audio-Rate Amplitude Envelope
* The temporal envelope follows the audio waveform, with modulation frequencies in the **300 Hz to 3.4 kHz** range (voice) or up to **5 kHz** (music broadcast).
* In the spectrogram, this appears as a continuous symmetric sideband structure mirroring the carrier.

---

## 🧬 Autocorrelation Periodicities

* Autocorrelation of the amplitude envelope may reveal periodicities related to the audio content, but these are **irregular and non-deterministic** (voice is not periodic at the packet level).
* No sharp clock-like autocorrelation peaks are expected (unlike digital modulations FSK, OOK, PSK).
* Weak, broad peaks at lags corresponding to speech pitch frequencies (~5-15 ms period for 70-200 Hz fundamental) may appear but should not be confused with digital symbol clocks.

---

## ⚠️ Common False Positives

### 1. SSB (Single Sideband) vs. DSB-AM
* SSB signals lack the strong center carrier spike. If no carrier peak is visible in the PSD, consider SSB rather than AM.
* AM has a characteristic **three-component PSD**: carrier + upper sideband + lower sideband.

### 2. OOK/ASK Digital Signals
* OOK/ASK also modulates amplitude, but produces **discrete on/off levels** (binary step transitions) rather than smooth continuous variation.
* Check the amplitude histogram: AM produces a **smooth, continuous distribution** while OOK shows **bimodal peaks** (at zero and carrier level).
