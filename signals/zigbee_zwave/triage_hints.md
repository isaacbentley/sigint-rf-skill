# Triage Hints: Zigbee (IEEE 802.15.4) & Z-Wave

These hints help the LLM identify and verify Zigbee and Z-Wave signals from statistical reports generated by `triage_iq.py`.

---

## 🔍 Key Spectral Indicators

### 1. Occupied Bandwidth (OBW)

| Signal | Band | 99% OBW |
|---|---|---|
| **Zigbee 2.4 GHz** | 2.405–2.480 GHz | **~2 MHz** (main lobe) |
| **Zigbee 868 MHz** | 868.3 MHz | **~600 kHz** |
| **Zigbee 915 MHz** | 902–928 MHz | **~1.2 MHz** |
| **Z-Wave (9.6/40 kbps)** | 908.42 / 868.42 MHz | **~200 kHz** |
| **Z-Wave (100 kbps)** | Same | **~300 kHz** |

#### Critical Differentiator: Zigbee vs Wi-Fi at 2.4 GHz
* Zigbee: **~2 MHz** occupied bandwidth.
* Wi-Fi (802.11b/g/n): **~16.25–20 MHz** occupied bandwidth.
* Zigbee is **10x narrower** than Wi-Fi — this is the primary visual/spectral discriminator in the 2.4 GHz band.

#### Critical Differentiator: Z-Wave vs LoRa at Sub-GHz
* Z-Wave: **~200 kHz** bandwidth at exactly **908.42 MHz** (US) or **868.42 MHz** (EU).
* LoRa: **125/250/500 kHz** bandwidth, but using CSS (chirp spread) not FSK.
* Z-Wave uses a single fixed frequency per region — no frequency hopping.

### 2. PAPR (Peak-to-Average Power Ratio)
* **Zigbee (O-QPSK with half-sine shaping)**: Near-constant envelope → PAPR **~1.5–3.0 dB**.
  - The half-sine pulse shaping creates MSK-like constant-envelope behavior.
* **Z-Wave (FSK)**: Constant envelope → PAPR **~1.0–2.5 dB**.
* If PAPR exceeds 5 dB, it is not a standard Zigbee or Z-Wave signal.

### 3. Spectral Shape
* **Zigbee 2.4 GHz**: DSSS spreading produces a smooth, bell-shaped ($\text{sinc}^2$-like) power spectral density with a clear main lobe (~2 MHz wide) and gradually falling sidelobes.
* **Z-Wave FSK**: Twin-peaked FSK spectrum with peaks at ±20 kHz deviation (classic) within ~200 kHz.
* OFDM flatness score will be **low (<0.4)** for both protocols.

## ⏱ Key Temporal Indicators

### 1. Packet Duration
* **Zigbee**: Very short packets.
  - Minimum (ACK frame, 5 bytes): $\approx 192\\ \mu\text{s}$ (preamble + SFD + data)
  - Maximum (127-byte payload): $\approx 4.256\\ \text{ms}$
  - Typical data packet: **0.5–2 ms**
  - If you see bursts longer than 5 ms on a single Zigbee channel, it's likely not Zigbee.

* **Z-Wave**: Longer packets due to lower data rate.
  - At 9.6 kbps: Preamble (8.33 ms) + 64-byte frame ≈ **60 ms**
  - At 40 kbps: Preamble (2 ms) + 64-byte frame ≈ **15 ms**
  - At 100 kbps: **~15 ms** including preamble

### 2. Duty Cycle
* Both Zigbee and Z-Wave are designed for **very low duty cycle** operation.
* Typical: **<0.1–1%** — devices transmit short bursts every few seconds to minutes.
* A continuously transmitting signal at these frequencies is almost certainly not Zigbee or Z-Wave.

### 3. Channel Hopping (Zigbee)
* Zigbee uses **fixed channel assignment** (not frequency hopping) within a network.
* However, multiple Zigbee networks in the same area may use different channels (11–26).
* If you see signals on multiple 5 MHz-spaced channels, it could be co-located Zigbee networks.

## 🧬 Autocorrelation Periodicities

### Zigbee DSSS Chip Periodicity
* During the preamble (symbol `0000` repeated 8 times), the DSSS sequence repeats every **32 chips**:
  - At 2 Mchip/s: chip period = $0.5\\ \mu\text{s}$
  - 32-chip sequence period: $32 \times 0.5\\ \mu\text{s} = 16\\ \mu\text{s}$
  - At 8 MSPS capture rate: autocorrelation peak at **128 samples**
  - At 4 MSPS capture rate: autocorrelation peak at **64 samples**

### Zigbee Symbol Periodicity
* Symbol period at 2.4 GHz: $1 / 62500 = 16\\ \mu\text{s}$ (same as 32-chip period, since 32 chips per symbol)

### Z-Wave Preamble Periodicity
* The alternating `01010101` preamble creates autocorrelation peaks at **2 bit periods**:
  - At 9.6 kbps: $2 \times 104.17\\ \mu\text{s} = 208.33\\ \mu\text{s}$
  - At 40 kbps: $2 \times 25\\ \mu\text{s} = 50\\ \mu\text{s}$

## ⚠️ Common False Positives

| Confounder | How to Distinguish |
|---|---|
| **Wi-Fi (802.11b/g/n) at 2.4 GHz** | Wi-Fi is 10x wider (~20 MHz vs ~2 MHz). Wi-Fi OFDM has high PAPR (8–12 dB). Even 802.11b (DSSS) uses 22 MHz bandwidth. |
| **Bluetooth at 2.4 GHz** | Bluetooth hops across 79 channels at 1 MHz spacing. Individual hops are ~1 MHz wide (narrower than Zigbee). Hopping rate is 1600 hops/sec. |
| **LoRa at Sub-GHz** | LoRa uses CSS (chirp) modulation, not FSK or DSSS. Spectrogram shows distinctive chirp sweep patterns. LoRa bandwidths are 125/250/500 kHz. |
| **ISM narrowband devices at 915/868 MHz** | Many proprietary ISM devices operate here. Check for Z-Wave's exact frequencies (908.42/868.42 MHz) and alternating-bit preamble. |
| **Thread (802.15.4)** | Thread uses the same IEEE 802.15.4 PHY as Zigbee — they are **indistinguishable at the PHY layer**. Differentiation requires MAC/network layer decoding. |

## 🎯 Quick Decision Tree

```
1. Band?
   ├─ 2.4 GHz (2.405–2.480 GHz)
   │   └─ OBW ~2 MHz? (NOT 20 MHz)
   │       └─ YES → Zigbee candidate
   │           └─ DSSS spreading visible (smooth sinc² spectrum)?
   │               └─ YES → Zigbee 2.4 GHz (IEEE 802.15.4)
   ├─ ~908 MHz (US) or ~868 MHz (EU)
   │   ├─ Exactly 908.42 / 868.42 MHz?
   │   │   └─ OBW ~200 kHz, FSK twin-peaked spectrum?
   │   │       └─ YES → Z-Wave
   │   └─ OBW ~600 kHz–1.2 MHz, BPSK-DSSS?
   │       └─ YES → Zigbee Sub-GHz (802.15.4)
   └─ Other
       └─ Not Zigbee or Z-Wave

2. Packet duration < 5 ms at 2.4 GHz? → Consistent with Zigbee
3. Alternating-bit preamble at 908/868 MHz, ~200 kHz BW? → Z-Wave
```
