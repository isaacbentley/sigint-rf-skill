# Triage Hints: ADS-B Mode S (1090 MHz)

These hints help the LLM identify and verify ADS-B Mode S signals from statistical reports generated by `triage_iq.py`.

---

## 🔍 Key Spectral Indicators

### 1. Frequency Band
* ADS-B operates strictly on **1090 MHz** for air-to-ground downlink. 
* Ground-to-air interrogation occurs at **1030 MHz** (using similar formats).
* If the center frequency of your capture is at 1090 MHz, ADS-B is the primary candidate.

### 2. Occupied Bandwidth (OBW)
* The OBW (99%) is consistently around **2.0 MHz to 2.5 MHz**.
* This is due to the fast $0.5\ \mu\text{s}$ pulse edges.

---

## ⏱ Key Temporal Indicators

### 1. Strict Burst Durations
ADS-B transponders emit highly constrained packets. Look for active burst durations of:
* **$120.0\ \mu\text{s}$** (Long ADS-B position report: 112 bits + 8 µs preamble).
* **$64.0\ \mu\text{s}$** (Short Mode S acquisition frame: 56 bits + 8 µs preamble).
* These bursts are extremely short compared to other packetized signals like Wi-Fi or LoRa.

### 2. Duty Cycle & PAPR
* Duty cycle is **extremely low (< 0.1%)** as aircraft only transmit occasionally to avoid channel congestion.
* The peak-to-average power ratio (PAPR) of the entire file will be **very high (> 12 dB)** because the file contains long silent intervals between short, high-power bursts.

---

## 🧬 Autocorrelation Periodicities

Because PPM modulation shifts pulses on $0.5\ \mu\text{s}$ boundaries (which are multiples of $0.5\ \mu\text{s}$ or $1.0\ \mu\text{s}$), the power envelope exhibits cyclic properties:
* Look for a peak at lag **$1.0\ \mu\text{s}$** (corresponding to the bit duration).
* At **2.0 MSPS**, this corresponds to a lag of **2 samples**.
* At **15.36 MSPS**, this corresponds to a lag of **15 samples**.
* Correlation peaks at these lags confirm high symbol transition symmetry, characteristic of PPM.
