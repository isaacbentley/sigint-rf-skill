# Triage Hints: AIS — Automatic Identification System (VHF Maritime)

These hints help the LLM identify and verify AIS signals from statistical reports generated by `triage_iq.py`.

---

## 🔍 Key Spectral Indicators

### 1. Frequency Band
* AIS operates on exactly **two frequencies**: **161.975 MHz** (Ch 87B) and **162.025 MHz** (Ch 88B).
* These are in the VHF maritime band (156–163 MHz). If your capture center frequency covers this range, AIS is a strong candidate.
* The two channels are separated by exactly **50 kHz**.

### 2. Occupied Bandwidth (OBW)
* Each AIS channel occupies approximately **16 kHz** (99% OBW) within a 25 kHz channel allocation.
* GMSK with BT = 0.4 at 9600 baud produces a compact, smooth spectral shape — no sharp sidelobes.
* The PSD profile shows a **rounded bell-curve** shape, distinct from sharper FSK or flat-topped OFDM.

### 3. Dual-Channel Signature
* AIS is one of the few protocols that uses exactly **two fixed narrowband channels** in the VHF maritime band.
* If your wideband capture shows two ~16 kHz-wide energy clusters centered on 161.975 and 162.025 MHz — **AIS is almost certain**.

---

## ⏱ Key Temporal Indicators

### 1. Slot Timing
* AIS transmissions align to a strict **26.667 ms TDMA slot** structure (2250 slots per minute).
* Look for burst spacing that is a multiple of ~26.7 ms.
* In busy ports, many consecutive slots will be occupied, creating near-continuous transmission on each channel.

### 2. Burst Duration
* A single-slot standard position report (168 data bits + HDLC overhead ≈ 256 bits total at 9600 baud) lasts approximately:
$$T_{burst} \approx \frac{256}{9600} \approx 26.7\ \text{ms}$$
* Multi-slot messages (Type 5) occupy exactly **2 consecutive slots** (~53.3 ms).
* Look for bursts that are **exact multiples of 26.7 ms**.

### 3. Duty Cycle
* In coastal areas near shipping lanes, duty cycle can be **moderate to high (10–60%)** due to vessel density.
* In open ocean, duty cycle drops to **very low (<1%)**.
* PAPR of the entire capture will be **low within active bursts** (constant-envelope GMSK) but **high globally** if many silent gaps exist.

---

## 🧬 Autocorrelation Periodicities

### Symbol-Level
* GMSK at 9600 baud produces a symbol period of:
$$T_{sym} = \frac{1}{9600} \approx 104.17\ \mu\text{s}$$
* At **48 kSPS**, this is a lag of **5 samples**.
* At **250 kSPS**, this is a lag of **26 samples**.
* A strong autocorrelation peak at the symbol period confirms 9600 baud.

### Slot-Level
* The TDMA slot period of **26.667 ms** should produce periodic activity:
  - At **48 kSPS**: lag of **1280 samples**.
  - At **250 kSPS**: lag of **6667 samples**.

## ❌ Common False Positives

| Confused With | How to Distinguish |
|---|---|
| **Marine VHF voice (Ch 16, etc.)** | Voice is analog FM with much wider bandwidth (~12 kHz deviation). AIS has constant 9600 baud data characteristics — no voice modulation patterns. |
| **POCSAG / Pager signals** | POCSAG is FSK at 512/1200/2400 baud — significantly lower baud rate. AIS is GMSK at exactly 9600 baud. |
| **DMR / dPMR** | DMR uses 4FSK at 4800 baud per slot on 12.5 kHz channels. AIS uses GMSK at 9600 baud on 25 kHz channels at specific frequencies. |
| **APRS (144.390 MHz)** | APRS uses AFSK at 1200 baud on a different frequency band entirely. |

## ✅ High-Confidence AIS Identification Checklist

1. ☐ Center frequency at or near 161.975 / 162.025 MHz
2. ☐ Two narrowband (~16 kHz) energy clusters separated by 50 kHz
3. ☐ Constant-envelope (low PAPR within bursts) — confirms GMSK
4. ☐ Baud rate measurement near 9600 (autocorrelation at ~104 µs)
5. ☐ Burst durations at multiples of ~26.7 ms
6. ☐ HDLC `0x7E` flags detected after NRZI decoding
