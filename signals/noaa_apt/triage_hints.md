# Triage Hints: NOAA APT & Meteor-M LRPT — Weather Satellite Imagery

These hints help the LLM identify and verify weather satellite signals from statistical reports generated by `triage_iq.py`.

---

## 🔍 Key Spectral Indicators

### 1. Frequency Band
* All weather satellite downlinks cluster tightly in the **137 MHz band** (137.0–138.0 MHz).
* **This band is highly distinctive** — very few other services operate here.
* Known frequencies:
  | Signal | Satellite | Frequency |
  |--------|-----------|-----------|
  | APT | NOAA 15 | 137.6200 MHz |
  | APT | NOAA 18 | 137.9125 MHz |
  | APT | NOAA 19 | 137.1000 MHz |
  | LRPT | Meteor-M2-3 | 137.9000 MHz |
  | LRPT | Meteor-M2-4 | 137.1000 MHz |

* **Caution**: NOAA 19 (137.1 MHz) and Meteor-M2-4 (137.1 MHz) share the same frequency — distinguish by modulation type (FM/AM vs. QPSK).

### 2. APT Bandwidth
* APT signals occupy approximately **34 kHz** (99% OBW).
* The PSD shows a **characteristic FM shape**: a broad, rounded spectral hump centered on the carrier frequency, with no sharp edges.
* A **prominent 2400 Hz subcarrier** may appear as spectral energy ±2.4 kHz from center in the demodulated audio spectrum.

### 3. LRPT Bandwidth
* LRPT signals are significantly **wider**: **100–140 kHz** (depending on symbol rate and filtering).
* The PSD shows a **flat-topped QPSK spectral shape** with steep rolloff edges — resembling a rectangular raised-cosine spectrum.
* Much more bandwidth than APT — this is the clearest APT vs. LRPT distinguisher.

### 4. Summary Comparison
| Feature | APT (NOAA) | LRPT (Meteor-M) |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| Bandwidth | ~34 kHz | ~100–140 kHz |
| Spectral shape | FM hump (rounded) | Flat-topped QPSK (steep edges) |
| Modulation | FM + AM subcarrier | QPSK |
| Data type | Analog image | Digital image |

## ⏱ Key Temporal Indicators

### 1. Pass Duration
* Weather satellite signals appear as **long, continuous transmissions lasting 8–15 minutes**.
* This is extremely distinctive — very few signals in the 137 MHz band are continuous for this duration.
* The signal rises from the noise floor, reaches maximum strength at culmination (highest elevation), and fades back into noise — a classic **bell-curve amplitude envelope**.

### 2. APT Line Timing
* APT transmits exactly **2 lines per second** (one visible + one infrared).
* Each line is 0.5 seconds, producing a highly periodic signal.
* The 2400 Hz subcarrier is **continuously present** during the entire pass — a constant "singing" tone.
* **Sync A** (1040 Hz, 39 pixels = 9.375 ms) and **Sync B** (different pattern) repeat every 0.5 seconds.

### 3. LRPT Timing
* LRPT transmits **continuously** during the entire pass — no bursty behavior.
* At 72 kBaud QPSK, the signal is a constant stream of symbols with no silent intervals.
* Frame sync (ASM `0x1ACFFC1D`) repeats every CADU (1024 bytes = 8192 bits → at 144 kbps, every ~56.9 ms).

### 4. Duty Cycle
* During a pass: **~100% duty cycle** — the satellite transmits continuously.
* Between passes: **0% duty cycle** — the satellite is below the horizon.
* If your capture spans an entire pass, expect a single continuous signal block of 8–15 minutes with smooth amplitude variation.

## 🧬 Autocorrelation Periodicities

### APT
* **Line period**: Strong autocorrelation peak at **0.5 seconds** (2 lines/sec).
  - At **48 kSPS** (audio): lag of **24,000 samples**.
  - At **250 kSPS** (RF): lag of **125,000 samples**.
* **Subcarrier period**: The 2400 Hz tone produces a period of **416.7 µs**.
  - At **48 kSPS**: lag of **20 samples**.
* **Sync A period**: 1040 Hz square wave repeating every 0.5 s (but the sync itself is only 9.375 ms long).

### LRPT
* **Symbol period**: At 72 kBaud, symbol duration is ~13.9 µs.
  - At **288 kSPS**: lag of **4 samples**.
  - At **1 MSPS**: lag of **14 samples**.
* **CADU frame period**: ~56.9 ms (at 144 kbps for 1024-byte frames).
  - At **288 kSPS**: lag of **16,389 samples**.

## 📡 Doppler Signature

### Critical Distinguisher
* Weather satellites in LEO (~830 km altitude) produce **significant Doppler shift**:
$$\Delta f_{max} \approx \pm 3.4\ \text{kHz at 137 MHz}$$
* The Doppler shift follows an **S-curve**: positive on approach, zero at closest approach, negative on recession.
* Total Doppler sweep across a pass: **~6.8 kHz** over 8–15 minutes.
* **This S-shaped frequency drift is a strong satellite identifier** — terrestrial signals do not exhibit this pattern.
* For APT (FM, 34 kHz BW): Doppler is manageable, ~10% of bandwidth.
* For LRPT (QPSK, 100+ kHz BW): Doppler is a smaller fraction of bandwidth, but still requires tracking for coherent demodulation.

## ❌ Common False Positives

| Confused With | How to Distinguish |
|---|---|
| **NOAA POES HRPT (1698/1707 MHz)** | HRPT is at L-band (~1700 MHz), not 137 MHz. Completely different frequency range. |
| **Amateur radio (144–146 MHz)** | Amateur band is 7+ MHz higher. Also typically bursty voice/data, not continuous 10+ minute transmissions. |
| **Pager systems (POCSAG/FLEX, ~148–174 MHz)** | Pager signals are narrowband FSK bursts, not continuous wideband FM or QPSK. Different frequency range and duty cycle. |
| **Military satellites (137 MHz band)** | Some military UHF SATCOM operates nearby. Distinguished by different modulation, bandwidth, and non-polar orbits. |
| **NOAA APT vs. Meteor LRPT on same frequency** | NOAA 19 (137.1 MHz) and Meteor-M2-4 (137.1 MHz) share a frequency. APT = ~34 kHz FM; LRPT = ~100+ kHz QPSK. Check bandwidth and modulation type. |
| **Terrestrial FM broadcast (88–108 MHz)** | FM broadcast is much lower in frequency and much wider bandwidth (~200 kHz). |

## ✅ High-Confidence Weather Satellite Identification Checklist

### For APT (NOAA):
1. ☐ Center frequency at 137.1000, 137.6200, or 137.9125 MHz
2. ☐ Occupied bandwidth ~34 kHz (FM spectral shape — rounded hump)
3. ☐ Continuous signal lasting 8–15 minutes with bell-curve amplitude envelope
4. ☐ Doppler S-curve of ±3.4 kHz across the pass
5. ☐ Demodulated audio contains a continuous **2400 Hz tone**
6. ☐ Strong autocorrelation at 0.5 s (line period = 2 lines/sec)
7. ☐ Sync A pattern (1040 Hz square wave) detected every 0.5 seconds

### For LRPT (Meteor-M):
1. ☐ Center frequency at 137.1000 or 137.9000 MHz
2. ☐ Occupied bandwidth ~100–140 kHz (flat-topped QPSK spectral shape)
3. ☐ Continuous signal lasting 8–15 minutes with bell-curve amplitude envelope
4. ☐ Doppler S-curve of ±3.4 kHz across the pass
5. ☐ QPSK constellation visible (4 clusters in IQ scatter plot)
6. ☐ Symbol rate near 72 kBaud (autocorrelation at ~13.9 µs)
7. ☐ ASM pattern `0x1ACFFC1D` detected after Viterbi decoding
